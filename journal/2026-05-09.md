## Reasoning

This is the first capital deployment. The mandate is high return / low volatility over a multi-year horizon, so I'm building a diversified core that I can incrementally tilt or rebalance in future runs rather than try to time a perfect entry on day one.

Allocation framework — one position per role, low-cost ETFs, whole-share friendly:

|---|---|---|---|
| VOO  | US large-cap core (S&P 500) | 48% | 47.47% |
| VXUS | International ex-US (developed + EM in one) | 14% | 13.93% |
| AVUV | US small-cap value factor tilt | 5%  | 4.91% |
| BND  | US investment-grade aggregate bonds | 20% | 19.93% |
| IAU  | Gold (inflation / regime diversifier) | 8%  | 8.00% |
| Cash | Buffer + optionality | 5%  | 5.76% |

Resulting exposure: ~66% equity / 20% bonds / 8% gold / ~6% cash. Three uncorrelated-ish risk drivers (equity, duration, real assets) plus a cash buffer. Tilts I deliberately included: small-cap value (AVUV) for a long-duration factor premium; gold (IAU) because over decades it has provided positive expected real return with low equity correlation, especially in inflationary regimes.

What I deliberately did **not** do:
- No single-stock picks. With $100k and no edge yet, ETFs dominate on Sharpe.
- No leverage, options, or sector bets.
- No emerging-markets standalone — VXUS already includes EM (~25%); a separate VWO sleeve would just fragment fees.
- No long Treasuries (TLT) sleeve — duration is already in BND; adding TLT would double-count and increase rate sensitivity for a small diversification gain.
- Did not deploy 100% — kept ~5.8% cash for next-run rebalancing flexibility and to avoid forced selling for fees.

## Notes for future me

- Yahoo Finance history pages are blocked by WebFetch (HTTP 503). `stockanalysis.com/etf/<ticker>/history/` works cleanly and was cross-checked against WebSearch for VOO. Use it as primary; cross-check at least one ticker each run against a second source (search snippet, Google Finance, or Nasdaq) before committing trades.
- Trade date in the ledger = the trading day whose close was used (Friday), not the run day (Saturday). `verify.py` enforces non-decreasing dates, so future Saturday runs will use the prior Friday and that's still monotonically increasing.
- Fee budget: at five trades per run we spend $10/week = ~$520/year ≈ 0.52% drag. Worth keeping trade count low; rebalance only when drift is meaningful (see MEMORY.md).
