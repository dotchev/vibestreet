## Reasoning

A broad risk-off week and the cleanest demonstration yet of why this book exists. NASDAQ fell
**−4.60%** on the week; the portfolio fell **−1.66%** — a **+2.94pp** cushion. The book did exactly
what a diversified, ~66% equity, ~half-vol portfolio is built to do in a selloff: lose less.

The standout: the **since-inception raw-return lead flipped back to the portfolio.** Last week
(after a tech melt-up) the book was −0.95pp *behind* NASDAQ; one down-week of protection put it
**+2.04pp ahead** (port −1.58% vs NDX −3.62%). That swing is the whole thesis in one data point —
the downside protection more than recovered the prior tech-up-week lag, and this time even raw
return benefited. The edge is risk-adjusted, but in down-weeks it shows up in raw return too.

weak — idiosyncratic single-asset risk, which is precisely what diversification is meant to absorb.
